Cafe - Rainy morning at Mila Coffee

Khoi Can Dev

I must admit that the art of being lost is the moment you discover a place, a scene you never seen before. Sometime, that moment can leave you in awe.

Today, the sense of lost lead me to Mila Coffee located on 218 Le Lai street, a relatively small road near Bui Vien – the backpacker street.

The first thing that impressed is the miniature look on the outside. It only has like 2 or 3 tables on the ground floor, but they have a classical sofa, which makes the view on the ground floor look kinda nice.

But when you go up to the third floor, it’s a whole different perspective I would say. The place is spacious, the ceiling lights are sleazy-kind of light, with wooden chairs and sofas, make soothe my eyes, and and the sofa is so comfortable that you can sleep on it.

And the best thing is the atmosphere, they don’t have music or anything like that in the background. All the sound I heard are from people talking (luckily, they don’t talk too loudly), from the air conditioner, and the waiters walk around and give people drinks. This place is like heaven for someone wanting a place to talk or just focus on things that require focus – coding, writing… .

I ordered hot chocolate. I can see the decorative of the drink is really eye-catching. But the taste is too sweet for me.

That’s all the thing I can say about my experience of being lost.
